March is Fire Prevention Month in the Philippines, which means it's a perfect time to discuss "Fire Safety". So, as regular citizens, what are some measures we can take to prevent fires in our homes?
Here are some tips in order to keep your family and house safe by making sure a fire never occurs in the first place;



How to Prevent Fires



Proper Storage of Gas Tanks1 		There are many households that use gas instead of electricity, especially when it comes to cooking food, which means we have to take this into account. Always check for gas leaks, and make sure to store in a well-ventilated area, so that, just in case there is a gas leak, gas accumulation will be avoided, and will lessen the chances of an explosive gas fire.

Dispose of Cigarette Buds Properly2 		Especially if you live in an area with a lot of foliage, like a garden, always make sure to dispose of cigarette buds properly, because if they are not put out, and are thrown into an area with highy flammable objects like dry leaves, it can cause fire. Aside from that, it will also pollute the environment.

Keep Flammable Objects Out Of Reach Of Children3 		Children, and by extension pets, often are not aware of the danger that certain household items like lit candles, match sticks, and lighters pose, which is why we should keep these out of their reach, for everyone's safety.

Make An Emergency Escape Plan4 		This is not limited to larger scaled buildings, but it is also good to think ahead just to be sure you and your family know what to do in case of fire. Make sure to make an emergency plan that contains all possible escape routes and a meeting spot to re-group with family after initial escape.

Say, despite all your precautions to avoid a fire, it happened anyway. What can you do in this situation?

How to Mitigate Fires



Close The Door5 		If you see that a fire has started in another room, but don't have any fire extinguishers nearby, immediately close the door, which provides a buffer before the fire spreads to the next room, giving more time to call for help and evacuate.

Extinguish The Fire Appropriately6 		If you have the capability to extinguish a fire, then immediately do so.

IMPORTANT NOTE
Make sure to take note of the following, and, if unsure, do not attempt extinguishing it.

  • Fires due to wood, paper, etc. -Extinguishing with water is sufficient
  • Fires due to oil or gasoline- Extinguish via cloth/"smothering the flames". DO NOT spray water on it; may just spread the substance, thus spreading the fire.
  • Fires due to electricity -Unplug the device causing smoke or fire if possible. DO NOT spray water either; possibility of getting shocked, aside from burnt.

Cover Face With Cloth7 		It makes sure that smoke and debris would not enter your respiratory tract, suffocating you. It also prevents flames from burning your face.

"Stop, Drop, And Roll"8 		If ever your clothes catch on fire, do not panic. Instead, follow the "Stop, Drop, and Roll" rule. By doing this, the fire will be extinguished because you will be cutting off its fuel source, Oxygen, by dropping and rolling.

Call Emergency Hotline9 		Once not in any immediate danger, immediately call emergency services to make sure the fire will not spread and become a larger problem.
